# User Tips for Effective Learning with Revise

Quick, actionable tips to get the most out of Revise's AI study planner and spaced repetition features.

---

## 📚 Creating Your Study Plan

### Tip 1: Upload Quality Study Materials
- Use detailed notes, textbooks (PDFs), or comprehensive outlines
- The AI generates better plans from richer content
- Include examples and definitions for better flashcards

### Tip 2: Be Realistic with Time
- Enter accurate available study time per day
- Better to study 30 minutes daily than 2 hours inconsistently
- The system adapts schedules to your availability

### Tip 3: Set Your Test Date Early
- The earlier you create your plan, the better
- Cramming works poorly with spaced repetition
- Aim for at least 2-3 weeks before your test date

---

## 🎯 Daily Study Sessions

### Tip 4: Review Due Cards First
- Open your Progress Dashboard
- Focus on flashcards marked "Due for review"
- These cards are optimally timed for retention

### Tip 5: Short, Consistent Sessions Beat Long Cramming
- 15-20 minutes daily > 2 hours once a week
- Spaced repetition requires consistency
- Use the Study Session page to track your progress

### Tip 6: Complete All Session Activities
- Do New Material, Flashcard review, AND Quizzes
- Each reinforces learning differently
- XP rewards encourage completion

---

## 💯 Flashcard Grading

### Tip 7: Rate Honestly
The algorithm depends on truthful ratings:
- **Again**: You forgot or got it wrong → Card returns tomorrow
- **Hard**: You struggled significantly → Card returns tomorrow
- **Good**: You recalled with some thought → Normal progression
- **Easy**: Instant, perfect recall → Faster progression

**Don't game the system**—honest ratings = better learning.

### Tip 8: Use "Again" Liberally
- It's better to over-review than under-review
- Don't feel bad about resetting cards
- Cards you struggle with need more repetitions

### Tip 9: Don't Review Too Early
- Trust the algorithm's scheduling
- Cards are due when you're about to forget
- Early reviews waste time without improving retention
- Exception: Last-minute exam prep

---

## 📊 Progress Tracking

### Tip 10: Check Your Dashboard Daily
- Monitor due card count
- See which topics need attention (low mastery)
- Celebrate progress with badges and XP

### Tip 11: Focus on Low-Mastery Topics
- Red/orange cards need more attention
- Green cards are well-learned
- Dashboard shows mastery breakdown per topic

### Tip 12: Maintain Your Streak
- Study at least 1 day in a row to start a streak
- Streaks unlock badges and motivation
- Consistency is key to long-term retention

---

## 🎮 Gamification & Motivation

### Tip 13: Set Daily XP Goals
- Completing sessions awards XP
- Track your XP over time on Progress page
- Challenge yourself to beat your own records

### Tip 14: Collect Badges
- Badges reward milestones (streaks, card reviews, mastery)
- View earned badges in the Progress Dashboard
- Each badge has specific unlock criteria

### Tip 15: Celebrate Small Wins
- Animations trigger for session completion
- XP gained and badges unlocked are shown in real-time
- Positive reinforcement keeps you motivated

---

## 🧠 Spaced Repetition Mastery

### Tip 16: Understand Easiness Factor (EF)
- EF determines interval growth speed
- Higher EF = longer intervals = stronger memory
- Ranges from 1.3 to 2.5

### Tip 17: Trust Long Intervals
- Cards can reach 30, 60, or 90+ day intervals
- This is normal and optimal for retention
- Don't manually review them early

### Tip 18: Reset Cards When Needed
- If you completely forget a card after a long break, rate it "Again"
- The system will re-train you on that card
- This is a feature, not a failure

---

## 🗓️ Study Calendar

### Tip 19: Plan Around Busy Days
- Use the Study Calendar to see upcoming reviews
- Anticipate high-due-count days
- Study a bit extra on light days to spread the load

### Tip 20: Review Before Exams
- A few days before your test, review ALL cards (not just due ones)
- Focus extra time on low-mastery topics
- The dashboard highlights weak areas

---

## 📈 Quiz Performance

### Tip 21: Use Quizzes to Test Understanding
- Flashcards test recall; quizzes test application
- Aim for 80%+ on quizzes before moving on
- Review incorrect answers carefully

### Tip 22: Take Quizzes Multiple Times
- Repetition solidifies learning
- Each quiz attempt is saved and tracked
- Watch your scores improve over time

---

## 🔧 Troubleshooting

### Tip 23: Too Many Due Cards?
- Happens if you created many cards at once
- Review what you can each day—system adapts
- Next time, spread card creation over multiple days

### Tip 24: Missing Days?
- Life happens—don't stress
- Due cards remain due until reviewed
- Jump back in and the system adjusts

### Tip 25: Bored with Easy Cards?
- Rate them "Easy" consistently
- They'll quickly reach long intervals (weeks/months)
- You'll rarely see them

---

## 🚀 Advanced Tips

### Tip 26: Batch Card Creation
- Create cards for multiple topics at once
- The system distributes reviews over time
- Avoids cramming

### Tip 27: Leverage the Study Calendar
- Visual overview of study activity
- Identify patterns (days you skip, days you excel)
- Adjust habits based on data

### Tip 28: Track Quiz Scores Over Time
- Progress Dashboard shows quiz performance chart
- Identify weak topics from quiz trends
- Revisit low-scoring areas

### Tip 29: Combine with Other Study Methods
- SM-2 is excellent for memorization
- Supplement with practice problems, essays, or discussions
- Flashcards are one tool in your learning toolkit

### Tip 30: Study Smarter, Not Harder
- The algorithm optimizes review timing
- Small, daily sessions are more effective than marathon cramming
- Trust the science—it works

---

## 💡 Remember

**Consistency > Intensity**

Spaced repetition rewards small, regular efforts over long, sporadic sessions. Study 15 minutes every day, rate cards honestly, and trust the algorithm. You'll be amazed at how much you retain.

**Happy learning! 🎓✨**
